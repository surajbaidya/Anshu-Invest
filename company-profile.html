company profile

With the main objective of providing institutional credit for enhancing the production and productivity of the agricultural sector in the country, the Agricultural Development Bank, Nepal was established in 1968 under the ADBN Act 1967, as successor to the cooperative Bank. The Land Reform Savings Corporation was merged with ADBN in 1973. Subsequent amendments to the Act empowered the bank to extend credit to small farmers under group liability and expand the scope of financing to promote cottage industries. The amendments also permitted the bank to engage in commercial banking activities for the mobilization of domestic resources.

The bank worked as a premier rural credit institution since its establishment, contributing substantial agricultural credit supply in the country. Rural finance has been the principal operational area of ADBN in the past. However, the bank is also involved in commercial banking operations since 1984, to provide commercial banking services. Stock Analysis of Nabil Bank Limited

As of: 2022/02/03 03:00

Nabil Bank (Nabil) has remained the leading financial institution since its establishment in 1984 AD. Nabil's net profit margin and return on equity in FY 2076/77 declined to 14.5 percent and 18.6 percent, mainly due to decrease net interest income. The Bank has a well-established risk management system with an NPA of 0.48 percent and 304.82 percent loan loss provisioning during Q3 2076/77. The Bank has 4.485 billion rupees investments in the public and promoter shares of Microfinance companies. As per the circular of NRB, banks should liquidate equity investments of microfinance companies within Poush 2078, expect investments to support and promote the deprived sector. It could significantly increase the non-operating revenue of the Bank. Competitive positioning, conservative growth strategy, and prudent underwriting practices solidify the asset quality of the Bank.

Off-balance sheet items represent 25 percent of the Bank's total assets in FY 2076/77. Commitments to low creditworthiness loans and low NPV projects could impact Banks' balance sheet strength in the future. After the mandatory provision of NRB to issue debentures, the Bank's financial leverage (asset/equity) has risen to 9.19 times in FY 2076/77 versus 8.67 in FY 2075/76. The increased unhealthy competition among banking and financial institutions (BFIs) with a focus on short-term goals could negatively affect the long-term solvency of BFIs. Nabil is among the larger banks in the industry regarding market share with a growing branch network, which remains favorable for future portfolio growth. The merger of Bank with United finance brings the post-merger synergy positively affecting profitability. However, the asset quality of finance companies is modest and could impair Bank's assets quality.

Stock Analysis of Nabil Bank Limited

The capital adequacy ratio (CAR) measures a bank's available capital expressed as a percentage of a bank's risk-weighted credit exposures. The substantial increase in credit risk exposures and market risk exposures compared to the rise in the total capital fund has declined the Bank's capital adequacy ratio. Nabil has maintained an adequate capitalization level (CRAR of 10.35% and CAR of 12.28% on Q3 2077/78, albeit slower than the industry average of ~10.71% and ~13.27%, respectively), supported by a healthy internal capital generation rate and profit retention in recent years. The well-positioned capital conservation buffer makes banks stronger at absorbing losses and provides a margin of safety during financial crises. The Central Bank's restriction on dividend distribution most likely supports cash conversation to Nabil. We expect future capitalization to remain adequate, given Nabil's reasonable rate of internal capital generation and track record of profit retention. The recent merger with United Finance Limited will have a positive contribution to the Bank's capitalization. A strong capital position reduces the likelihood of insolvency and boosts public trust in the Bank.

Assets Quality

Assets quality pertains to the amount of existing and potential credit risk associated with the Bank's assets focused primarily on financing assets. The loan portfolio constitutes the most significant portion of the bank assets. The loan quality depends on the creditworthiness of the borrowers and the corresponding risk weightage across the loans. The Bank has a current account and saving account to total deposits (CASA) mix of 58 percent less than the three-year average CASA mix of 60 percent. Such a high CASA proportion in total deposits has lowered its cost of funds (interest expenses), enhancing its base rate. It increases the Bank's competitive positioning in the base rate plus regime, attracting a good borrower profile.
